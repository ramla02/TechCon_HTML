History

TechCon began in 1998 as a small gathering of developers in San Francisco and has since evolved into a globally recognized technology conference, drawing thousands of innovators, entrepreneurs, and tech leaders each year. Initially focused on open-source software and web development, the event expanded rapidly through the 2000s, embracing topics like AI, cloud computing, and cybersecurity. By 2012, it had gone international, and during the COVID-19 pandemic, it transitioned to a fully virtual format, reaching a record-breaking global audience. Today, TechCon continues to lead conversations around emerging tech, ethical innovation, sustainability, and inclusivity—firmly establishing itself as both a reflection of technological progress and a driving force behind its future.

Mission

At TechCon, our mission is to empower innovation, foster inclusive collaboration, and ignite meaningful conversations that shape the future of technology. We are driven by a commitment to ethical advancement, global cooperation, and sustainable progress. Through open dialogue, knowledge sharing, and a celebration of diverse perspectives, we strive to create a tech community that builds solutions for real-world impact and a better tomorrow.

Past Speakers

Dr. Maya Bennett Dr. Maya Bennett is a renowned AI researcher and ethics advocate, celebrated for her pioneering work at MIT’s Media Lab on eliminating algorithmic bias. With over a decade of experience in machine learning and responsible AI development, she has advised global tech policy groups and spoken at major platforms including TED and the UN. Maya is committed to ensuring that AI technologies are fair, transparent, and designed with human dignity at the core.
